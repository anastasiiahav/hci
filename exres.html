Research Exertion Interfaces

During this topic I had to make some research as well. I have looked for two new examples of exertion interfaces that have not been mentioned during the lectures.

Remote Impact

Remote Impact or in other words "Sports over a Distance". Itâ€™s a game that provides a full body contact experience between multiple players on wide geographical distances.
The purpose of this interface is to encourage physical exertion, to eliminate the stress and to help people socialize and make new friends all over the world, while doing sports.
Remote Impact is a console game what recognizes and registers intense force. It does support generic messaging and business-oriented tasks. A silhouette of the remote participant is projected on the wall with a mattress standing against it. How harder you hit the matrass, how more points you get. The players can also talk with each other through the voice connection.

Table Tennis for Three

Table Tennis for Three is a three players table tennis-like game. The concept is similar to Remote Impact as it also provides a way to play with different people all over the world. The players use an Exertion Interface to create a social connection between each other. They do that so by communicating through an augmented videoconference.
The purpose of this game is socializing. It promotes positive moods and it can help overcome loneliness.
Each player has a table tennis table, a paddle and a ball. The table is set up so that the ball can be hit against the vertically positioned opposite half of the table. The backboards of the tables can detect the time and approximate location of a ball striking the table. There are eight piezoelectric sensors that are attached to the rear on the front of the backboard. The sensors detecting the sound vibrations from a ball striking.
